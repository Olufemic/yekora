The mechanics

How an auction runs, start to finish

Every auction on Yekoraa follows the same automated path — from the moment it goes live to the moment funds land in a seller's bank account.

Auction goes live

Timer countdown begins instantly once a listing clears the automated category rules.

Bidding phase

Real-time bid updates. Any bid landing in the final 60 seconds automatically extends the clock — anti-sniping by design.

Auto close

The moment time expires with no new bids, the system selects the highest bidder as winner and notifies both sides.

Payment window opens

The winner has a set window to pay via Paystack. Miss it, and the auction is cancelled outright — it does not pass to the next bidder, closing off a common collusion scheme.

Dispatch & tracking

Seller uploads dispatch photos, books Sendbox/GIG in-app, and both sides track the shipment live.

Escrow release

Funds release automatically once the buyer confirms delivery, or after the confirmation window expires — 48 hours locally, 5–10 days interstate.

Revenue model

Phase 1 keeps things simple on purpose: commission is the only active revenue stream, to remove every barrier to seller adoption while the platform builds audience.

Phase 1 — Active now

3–5% commission

Deducted from seller payout on every completed sale. Rises to 5–8% in Phase 2.

Phase 1 — Active now

Free listings

Unlimited listings per seller, no caps, no fees — removes friction from onboarding.
